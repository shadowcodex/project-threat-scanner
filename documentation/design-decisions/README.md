# Design Decisions

This is mostly human written. Poorly written. 

Typically brain to paper decisions.

These documents are things and thoughts and decisions that typically aren't directly discoverable in code. This is context that is valuable to agentic coding harnesses, the rest they can figure out from code.

Design decisions rarely change, much more static and easier to keep up to date than exact docs on how everything works.

Curious how to use a feature? Curious how something works? Ask an agent to read the code and tell you.


## Filling the gaps...

AI Agents are good these days. Having them write documentation on architecture etc... it's cool... good for humans to read... but the agents were able to find that out already to write the docs... so they could do it again... You are adding reinforcement of that and extra context, and potential to poison and rot your own context.

Instead you should have opinionated decisions. Things that fill the gaps of what is there, and why... You should put information down from your brain on the decisions you made to build your repo.

So you'll see that. Short and sweet guidance on why things exist the way they do in our folder here... 

These are the things that you yell at an AI and say "NO YOU IDIOT YOU WERE SUPPOSED TO DO XYZ BECAUSE OF ABC".. the ABC goes in here...

Reference it in your CLAUDE.md and stop asking the Agent to fill its own logic thinking gaps, and put your human brain to work to guide it.
